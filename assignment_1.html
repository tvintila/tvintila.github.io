"Net" response patterns for similar but distinct tasks under fMRI


  1. Highly specialized structure-function relationships in the brain may be mapped by comparing data sets from similar but distinct task paradigms. This experimental procedure would be set primarily for higher level perceptual processing- such as scene perception- which take in multiple streams of information and can be especially confounded to details of particular tasks. For example, a visual object recognition task could produce responses to photographs of a vase as well as cartoon pictures of a vase. These responses might be overlaid to pinpoint an abstracted value of "vase" recognition. Similar extensions could be made to distinguish generalizations and specifications (eg. vessels or cups), varied context influences on recognition, and other abstracted associations to the target. Of particular interest would be responses at the boundary where assured visual recognition is subject to uncertainty- setting minimal cues for full response of recognition.

  2. Data can be collected through recognition task paradigms discussed above. Some data sets for similar studies (lacking abstracted comparison) may be available for preprocessing and determining best presentation of target objects.

  3. An algorithmic model could be trained on participant responses between presentation types to identify specific response patterns according to target objects. At this point in the course I am not sure of which particular method would be best.

  4. As noted in b, current paradigms for object recognition may be processed and adapted to develop appropriate experimental procedures.
